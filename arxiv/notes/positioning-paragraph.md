# Positioning Paragraph

The recent embodied-robotics literature includes several explicit fast/slow or dual-system control architectures, particularly in vision-language-action models that separate slower semantic interpretation from faster low-level motor generation. The present work aligns with that broader direction but makes a different contribution. Rather than introducing a new monolithic robot foundation model, it explores how reactive and supervisory layers may be instantiated on a distributed asynchronous substrate, exercised functionally in `SOFTSIM`, and related to physical realization on `GA144`, a distributed asynchronous many-core or multi-node computing substrate. In this sense, the novelty of the work lies less in proposing fast/slow embodied control as an abstract idea, and more in providing an executable implementation framework for studying the cadence between those layers.
